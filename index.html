  • About
    dbumford@ .edu

    I am a third year PhD student in the Linguistics Department at New York University, working with Chris Barker. My research concentrates on formal semantics and pragmatics, especially in their interfaces with theoretical computer science. Most recently I've been thinking about quantification and pair-lists in dynamic semantics, but I have also worked on neg-raising, superlatives, and statistical grammar learning. Before that I was involved in a collaborative project investigating exhaustivity implicatures, clefts, and exclusives across languages.

    As an undergraduate at the University of Texas, I studied a lot of math, psychology, linguistics, and philosophy under the aegis of the Plan II Honors program. My thesis, supervised by Hans Kamp, was on synaesthetic metaphors—'loud colors', 'sour sounds', and the like—which I still think are a lot of fun.
